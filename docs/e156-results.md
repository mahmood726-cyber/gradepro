# GRADEPro: A Browser-Based GRADE Evidence Profile and Summary of Findings Generator — Results

Results: GRADEPro correctly computes certainty across all three embedded validation examples. For VTE anticoagulation versus warfarin (six RCTs, n=27,023), intracranial haemorrhage achieves HIGH certainty after a large-effect upgrade (RR 0.44, 95% CI 0.26 to 0.74; 3 fewer events per 1,000). Recurrent VTE and all-cause mortality are rated MODERATE following an imprecision downgrade; CIs cross null. Major bleeding is MODERATE owing to inconsistency (I-squared 52%). For COVID-19 corticosteroids (seven RCTs, n=7,898), 28-day mortality achieves HIGH certainty (RR 0.80, 95% CI 0.70 to 0.92; 50 fewer deaths per 1,000). Mechanical ventilation is MODERATE due to suspected publication bias (Egger p=0.08). For exercise in depression (28 RCTs, n=2,264), severity reduction is rated LOW following three downgrades partially offset by a dose-response upgrade (SMD minus-0.62, 95% CI minus-0.81 to minus-0.42). Auto-suggestions correctly flagged nine imprecision concerns, four inconsistency signals, and five publication bias warnings. All five export functions validated successfully. The application operates entirely offline without installation or server infrastructure.
